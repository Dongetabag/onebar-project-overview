The Experience

A website that lives the same rhythm as the room.

Most hospitality sites look the same at 11 AM and 11 PM. One Bar's won't. Recipe Labs is proposing a living website that flips from Day Mode to Night Mode on a schedule tied to Hartford's clock — a digital mirror of what walking in at 2 in the afternoon versus 10 on a Friday actually feels like. The overview you're reading is already running it. Try the toggle in the top nav.
6 AM → 6 PM · Hartford

Day Mode: the brunch, the lunch meeting, the kitchen.

Natural, cream, warm. A daytime site built to convert the Hartford professional grabbing lunch near Union Station, the private-event planner scoping rooms, the brunch crowd on Sunday at 1.

  • Reservation-first hero and brunch-forward menu
  • Private event inquiries surface in the top half
  • Team meetings, masterclasses, corporate bookings
  • Warm photography, lighter type, cleaner UI
6 PM → 6 AM · Hartford

Night Mode: the experience, the DJ, the energy.

Deep black, crimson glow, cinematic. The moment the room shifts into R&B Fridays, Round Robin, the Comedy Experience — the site shifts with it. Guests feel the tone before they open the door.

  • Tonight's event takes the hero
  • DJ lineup, cover, and countdown front and center
  • Signature cocktails, bottle service, private table booking
  • Cinematic reels auto-play, dim UI chrome

Auto-switching, overridable, persistent

The theme reads Hartford local time and flips automatically. Guests who prefer one mode can lock it with a single click and it remembers them. Staff can trigger Night Mode early for a special event. Every component, color, and image is tied into the system so the whole brand breathes with the hour.
